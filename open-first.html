Welcome to the

Student Onboarding Process!

Step 1:

Download Chrome Browser and sign-in.

  1. Follow this link to the Chrome website.
  2. Click "Download here" and follow the on-screen prompts.
  3. Find the downloaded file and double-click to install.
  4. After installation, open Chrome from your applications.
  5. Access Settings: Click the three-dot menu > "Settings".
  6. Scroll down, click "Sign in to Chrome," and enter the Gmail credentials you had just created.
  7. Choose what you want to sync, then click "Turn on".
  8. Enable Syncing (optional): Go to Settings > Sync and click "Sync".

Step 2:

Create An Email Address To Use As A Developer.

Optionally you can use your personal email address or another existing email address (it is recommended that you start a new email address).

  1. Follow this link to the Gmail website.
  2. Click "Create account", It's next to the sign-in option.
  3. Enter your name, desired email address, password, birthdate, and optionally your gender.
  4. Note down the Email address and Password you are about to create for later use.
  5. Google will send a code to your phone for verification.
  6. Accept Google's terms of service and privacy policy.
  7. Complete the CAPTCHA: Verify that you're not a robot.
  8. Click "Next".
  9. Your account is created!

Step 3:

Return to the folder you started from and double click on 'required software'.

To unzip for Windows:

  1. Right-click on the zipped folder named 'Windows'.
  2. Select "Extract All" from the submenu.
  3. Choose the extraction location: For now ignore this and just click 'extract'.
  4. Wait for the extraction to complete.
  5. There should now be a normal looking folder named 'Windows'.
  6. Double-click to open it.

To unzip for Mac:

  1. Double-click on the zipped folder named 'Mac'.
  2. The folder will automatically extract its contents.
  3. There should now be a normal looking folder named 'Mac'.
  4. Double-click to open it.

Step 4:

Follow these steps to sign up for a GitHub account:

  1. Follow this link to the Github website.
  2. Once the page loads, youâ€™ll see a green button labeled "Sign up for GitHub" on the right side of the page. Click on it.
  3. Enter a unique username, your email address, and a strong password.
  4. GitHub will send you a verification email to the address you provided. Open the email and click on the verification link.
  5. Select the free plan (recommended) or a paid plan based on your needs (you can ignore this for now).
  6. Complete the CAPTCHA: Verify that you're not a robot.
  7. Set up your profile (optional): Add a profile picture, bio, and any other information you'd like to share.
  8. Once you're signed up, you can start using GitHub for version control, collaboration, and more!

Step 5:

Follow these steps to join FreeCodeCamp:

  1. Follow this link to the freecodecamp website.
  2. Sign up: Click on the "Sign in / Sign up" button at the top right corner of the page, then follow the prompts to create an account.
  3. Complete the curriculum: Once signed in, you can start learning by completing the interactive coding challenges and projects available on the platform.
  4. Get certified: FreeCodeCamp offers free certifications in various programming disciplines. Work through the curriculum to earn certifications that can boost your resume.
  5. Connect with the community: Join forums, chat rooms, and social media groups associated with FreeCodeCamp to connect with other learners and mentors.
